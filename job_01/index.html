Bangladesh Liberation War of 1971

Historical archives of the 1971 Bangladesh Liberation War provide public access to millions of digitized documents,photographs, rare audio files, and video records documenting the birth of Bangladesh.The Bangladesh Liberation War of 1971 was a nine-month armed conflict that resulted in the secession of East Pakistan and the establishment of the independent People's Republic of Bangladesh. The war began on 26 March 1971 and ended with a decisive victory for the allied forces of Bangladesh and India on 16 December 1971.

featured highlights

The featured exhibits within the 1971 war archives highlight the deeply personal and global dimensions of the conflict,bridging civilian sacrifice with international diplomacy.

Important Document

The structural and legal foundation of Bangladesh is preserved across several critical wartime documents. Archives highlight three foundational items that legally established the country and brought a formal end to the 1971 war.

Important Interviews

Oral history archives of the 1971 war preserve critical interviews from military commanders, political leaders, international journalists, and frontline freedom fighters. These recorded testimonies provide firsthand accounts of strategic decisions and the human cost of the conflict.

Important Photographs

Photographs from the 1971 Bangladesh Liberation War served as irreplaceable, raw documentation that pierced through military blackouts to expose atrocities and capture the fierce spirit of the resistance. Archival collectives, such as the Muktijuddho e-Archive and the Liberation War Museum, categorize several historic visual frames as definitive icons of the war.
