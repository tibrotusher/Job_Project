Our Brave heroes Testimonials

Captain Mohiuddin Jahangir

The only Captain on the list. He orchestrated a daring escape from West Pakistan to join Sector 7 and died fighting while destroying enemy machine-gun bunkers in Chapai Nawabganj.

Sepoy Mohammad Hamidur Rahman

Sepoy Mohammad Hamidur Rahman is revered as the youngest recipient of the Bir Sreshtho, the highest military gallantry award in Bangladesh. He sacrificed his life at the age of 18 during a critical operation.

Flight Lieutenant Matiur Rahman

Flight Lieutenant Matiur Rahman was a pilot in the Bangladesh Air Force (and erstwhile Pakistan Air Force) who was posthumously awarded the Bir Sreshtho, Bangladesh's highest military honor, for his ultimate sacrifice during the 1971 Liberation War.

Sepoy Mohammad Mostafa Kamal

Sepoy Mohammad Mostafa Kamal was a fearless infantryman of the Bangladesh Army who was posthumously awarded the Bir Sreshtho for his exceptional bravery and self-sacrifice while saving his entire unit during the 1971 Liberation War

Lance Naik Munshi Abdur Rouf

Lance Naik Munshi Abdur Rouf was a fearless soldier of the erstwhile East Pakistan Rifles who was posthumously awarded the Bir Sreshtho for single-handedly halting an enemy fleet and saving over 150 fellow freedom fighters during the 1971 Liberation War.

Lance Naik Nur Mohammad Sheikh

Lance Naik Nur Mohammad Sheikh was a legendary soldier of the erstwhile East Pakistan Rifles who was posthumously awarded the Bir Sreshtho. He chose to stay behind while heavily wounded, sacrificing his own life to provide covering fire so his team could safely escape an enemy ambush during the 1971 Liberation War.

The Bangladesh Liberation War, which occurred in 1971, was a nine-month armed conflict between Bengali nationalists in East Pakistan (now Bangladesh) and the Pakistani military junta based in West Pakistan.
